# Mindset Calibration

Internal rule:
"I am not trying to impress. I am showing how I think and build."

Goal: calm confidence — remove nervous energy and over-explaining.

Remove nervous energy
- Breathe: take 3 slow, diaphragmatic breaths before you speak.
- Anchor phrase: silently repeat the internal rule to recenter.
- Slow your pace: deliberately pause 1–2s between sentences.
- Physical: rest your hands, keep shoulders relaxed, avoid fidgeting.

Remove over-explaining
- BLUF first: give the one-sentence answer (Bottom Line Up Front).
- If asked: provide one supporting fact or metric, then stop.
- Offer: "If helpful, I can go into the technical details." Let the interviewer request depth.

Practice drills (3x)
1. Say the internal rule aloud, breathe, deliver a 30s BLUF, pause, then one supporting sentence.
2. Record 3 takes; pick the cleanest with the fewest fillers.
3. Use the `/delivery` recorder to check for filler words and pacing.

Before any answer (quick checklist)
1. Breathe (3 slow breaths)
2. Say the one-line BLUF
3. Pause 1s
4. Give 1 supporting sentence (optional)
5. Stop and ask if they'd like details

Short scripts to memorize
- "I am not trying to impress; I'm showing how I think and build."
- BLUF starter: "Bottom line: [one-sentence summary]."
- Follow-up starter: "If helpful, I can show the implementation details or metrics."
