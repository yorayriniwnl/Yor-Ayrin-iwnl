# Application strategy — Quick, high-quality outreach

Purpose: send concise, recruiter-friendly messages that include portfolio, GitHub, and resume — and mention a relevant project.

Always send
- Portfolio: replace `<PORTFOLIO_URL>` with your live site (e.g. https://your-site.example)
- GitHub: https://github.com/yorayriniwnl
- Resume (PDF link or attachment): `<RESUME_URL>` (e.g. https://your-site.example/resume.pdf)

How to use
- Pick the single most relevant project for the role (Yor Zenith for systems, AI Detector for ML, Neo Sculpt for 3D/interactive).
- Keep subject short and message 2–3 lines max. End with a clear CTA (15‑minute call / next steps).
- Attach `resume.pdf` for email; paste links for LinkedIn or application forms.

Subject line tips
- Use role + name + one keyword: e.g. "Frontend Engineer — Ayush Roy — React / Yor Zenith"

Quick sending checklist
- Replace placeholders: company name, role, and `<PORTFOLIO_URL>` / `<RESUME_URL>`.
- Attach resume PDF for email; include links in forms/LinkedIn.
- Mention the single most relevant project (one short phrase).

Follow-up
- If no reply in 5 business days, send a polite one‑sentence follow-up referencing the earlier message and asking about next steps.

Tracking
- Keep a simple spreadsheet (Company | Role | Contact | Message sent | Date | Follow-up date | Outcome).

Notes
- Short, specific, and relevant beats long and generic. Quality over quantity.
